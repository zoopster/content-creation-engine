# Brand Voice Guidelines

## Core Voice Attributes

### Professional Yet Approachable
- Use clear, direct language
- Avoid jargon unless writing for technical audiences
- Be authoritative without being condescending
- Show expertise through insight, not buzzwords

### Data-Driven
- Support claims with evidence
- Cite sources when appropriate
- Use specific numbers over vague terms
- Present balanced perspectives

### Action-Oriented
- Focus on outcomes and results
- Use active voice
- Provide clear next steps
- Emphasize practical application

## Writing Principles

### Clarity First
✅ **Do**:
- Use simple, precise language
- Define technical terms on first use
- Break complex ideas into digestible parts
- Use examples to illustrate points

❌ **Don't**:
- Use unnecessary jargon
- Create long, convoluted sentences
- Assume reader knowledge
- Bury the main point

### Consistency Matters
✅ **Do**:
- Maintain consistent terminology
- Follow style guide for formatting
- Use parallel structure in lists
- Keep tone steady throughout

❌ **Don't**:
- Switch terms for the same concept
- Mix formal and casual tone
- Vary formatting arbitrarily
- Change perspective (I, we, you) without reason

### Reader-Focused
✅ **Do**:
- Address reader needs directly
- Use "you" to engage readers
- Anticipate and answer questions
- Provide actionable takeaways

❌ **Don't**:
- Write about yourself excessively
- Ignore reader context
- Assume one-size-fits-all
- Leave readers without next steps

## Tone by Content Type

### Articles & Blog Posts
- **Tone**: Educational, conversational
- **Perspective**: Second person (you) or third person
- **Style**: Informative with practical examples
- **Length**: Medium sentences (12-18 words)

### Whitepapers & Reports
- **Tone**: Professional, authoritative
- **Perspective**: Third person
- **Style**: Evidence-based, structured
- **Length**: Varied sentences for rhythm

### Social Media
- **Tone**: Conversational, engaging
- **Perspective**: First person plural (we) or second person (you)
- **Style**: Concise, punchy, with clear CTA
- **Length**: Short sentences (8-12 words)

### Email
- **Tone**: Friendly, helpful
- **Perspective**: Second person (you)
- **Style**: Scannable, action-oriented
- **Length**: Short sentences and paragraphs

### Presentations
- **Tone**: Confident, clear
- **Perspective**: First person plural (we)
- **Style**: Bullet points, minimal text
- **Length**: Very short (5-10 words per point)

## Grammar & Mechanics

### Preferred Constructions
- **Active voice**: "We developed the solution" not "The solution was developed"
- **Parallel structure**: "fast, reliable, and secure" not "fast, with reliability, and security"
- **Strong verbs**: "optimize" not "make better"
- **Specific nouns**: "customers" not "people"

### Punctuation
- Use Oxford comma: "A, B, and C"
- Em dashes for emphasis—like this
- Colons to introduce lists or explanations
- Avoid excessive exclamation points!

### Numbers
- Spell out one through nine, use numerals for 10+
- Use commas in large numbers: 1,000 not 1000
- Spell out percentages in formal content: "50 percent" or "50%"
- Round to meaningful precision: "approximately 47%" not "47.3421%"

## Vocabulary Guidance

### Encouraged Words
- **Action**: implement, optimize, streamline, achieve, create
- **Quality**: effective, proven, reliable, innovative, comprehensive
- **Impact**: transform, improve, enhance, accelerate, enable
- **Clarity**: clear, specific, straightforward, transparent

### Words to Avoid
- **Clichés**: game-changing, revolutionary, cutting-edge, paradigm shift
- **Vague**: easy, simple, best, great, nice
- **Overused**: leverage, synergy, bandwidth, circle back
- **Diminutive**: just, actually, really, simply (when not adding meaning)

### Inclusive Language
- Use "they/them" for singular when gender unknown
- Say "people with disabilities" not "disabled people"
- Use "chair" or "moderator" not "chairman"
- Avoid assumptions about race, gender, age, ability
- Use "they" as singular pronoun when gender is unknown

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing content, verify:
- [ ] Tone matches content type
- [ ] No avoided terms present
- [ ] Active voice used (>85% of sentences)
- [ ] Sentences average < 20 words
- [ ] Paragraphs are scannable (3-5 sentences)
- [ ] Technical terms defined
- [ ] Examples support key points
- [ ] Call to action is clear
- [ ] Flesch Reading Ease > 60
- [ ] Brand vocabulary used consistently
